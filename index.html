Screen 5 of 12 — Sender Agent Assigned

What is an Agent?

👤 Agents are accredited bank customers with available liquidity who have consented to participate in the mPOS network. They are not employees or external companies. They earn a share of the 1% fee per transaction.

What the Engine Checks — Sender Side

💡 For the sender agent, the engine queries and locks the agent's international ledger balance only — confirming they have sufficient ledger value to facilitate this transaction.

Real Time Scan — Nigeria

🔐 Security Protocol — Random Assignment Active
⚠️ Neither the sending customer nor the agent can select each other. The engine scans accredited Nigeria agents with a qualifying international ledger balance at the moment of the transaction — then randomly assigns one. This ensures ledger value stays in active circulation and prevents coordinated transaction routing.

How Agents Recover Fiat

💡 After receiving international ledger from a transaction, agents must convert it back to local fiat within 3 days. The engine scans same-country agents and facilitates a local exchange. Agent A buys the ledger with fiat. Agent B sells the ledger and recovers cash. Both agents benefit. No money crosses the border.

LEDGER & AUTO-CONVERSION

International Ledger — Balance & 3-Day Cycle

Screen 11 of 12 — Ledger Update & Safety Mechanism

What is the International Ledger?

💡 The international ledger is a digital accounting record — not a separate currency, not a pool of money, not a reserve. Ledger balances are denominated in the agent's own local currency and exist solely for facilitating international transactions. The value never leaves the country. It is consumed as transactions are facilitated and must be replenished by converting back to fiat.
